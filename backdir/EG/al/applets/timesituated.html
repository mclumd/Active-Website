Time-situated reasoning

Many active logic applications depend on the time situatedness of the logic. These examples depend directly on the logic knowing what time it is as it executes.
In all of these examples, note that the times (step) at which we want the actions to happen and the times they actually do are different. This is because that straightforward way to axiomatize the behavior does not take into account the delay in doing the inference for the action to occur. It would be a simple matter to adjust the axioms to do so, but a more interesting approach would be to get the logic itself to notice that, for example we request that "lunch" be asserted at step 10, but it only does so at step 11, and modify its behavior accordingly.
