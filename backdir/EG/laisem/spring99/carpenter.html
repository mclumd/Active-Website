Title: "Type-Logical Grammar"

Bob Carpenter
Bell Labs


Abstract:

In this talk, I will show how type-logical categorial grammar solves a number of previously intractable linguistic issues in a novel, elegant and general way. I will introduce Lambek's seminal 1958 syntactic calculus, which provides the logical insights underlying type-logical grammars. I will then use core linguistic issues to work up to the state-of-the-art multimodal type-logical grammars, placing type-logical grammars in a rich landscape of substructural logics. Concrete linguistic issues addressed include: quantifier scope, reciprocation and reflexivization, unbounded dependencies: relativization and pied piping, 'non-constituent' coordination, locality and boundednss, islands, coordination of 'unlike categories', and partially-free word order. I contend that the type-logical analyses of these phenomena are the simplest, most comprehensive and most general available.
