TITLE: ``Magic set computations in deductive DBs''

ABSTRACT:

In this talk, we introduce a proof method for verifying partial and total correctness of deductive databases, and apply it to the study of the ``magic-sets'' transformation. As a result, a logical reconstruction of efficient bottom-up execution of goal-driven deduction is accomplished, in the sense that the correctness of the transformation is obtained by purely declarative reasoning, abstracting away from procedural semantics.

Brief Resume:

D. Pedreschi is an Associate Professor of Computer Science at the at the CS Department of the University of Pisa, where he received his Ph.D. in 1987. His main research interests include logic programming and deductive databases, formal methods for program verification, semantics and implementation of programming languages, object-oriented databases. He has been the scientific coordinator of various national, European and joint EEC-NSF research projects in the area of logic programming and deductive databases. He is currently the chair of the undergraduate program in Computer Science at the Univ. of Pisa.
