Title: "Elements of a Computational Theory of Dialog"

Syed S. Ali
Natural Language and Knowledge Representation Research Group
University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee


Abstract:
Intelligent dialog systems (IDS) are concerned with the effective management of an incremental, interactive, user-system interaction (a dialog). An IDS must deal with both input and output (interpretation and presentation) and must monitor the effectiveness of its actions. To be effective, computer systems must facilitate users' understanding (for training and education) by incrementally presenting only the most relevant information, by evaluating users' understanding, and by adapting the interaction to address communication problems as they arise.

The general goal of my work is to investigate computational models of dialog that can support effective interaction between people and computer systems. I am doing so by providing a specification and representation of information that influence how people understand and respond in an ongoing dialog. My research also aims to provide computational methods for integrating and using this information to produce relevant utterances and to identify and resolve communication problems as they arise.

This talk will overview some results of our research group's work and its application to the task of improving health care education.

This is joint work with Susan McRoy, Songsak Channarukul, and Angelo Restificar.
