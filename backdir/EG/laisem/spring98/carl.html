Title: "Gardenfors' Theory of Belief Revision"

Carl Andersen


Abstract: This lecture summarizes Peter Gardenfors' book Knowledge in Flux, which presents seminal work by Gardenfors, Alchourron, and Makinson on belief revision. Belief revision concerns the rules by which rational agents change their beliefs in the presence of new information. Gardenfors argues that minimal, consistent change to the agent's belief system is the primary criterion of whether the agent's revision is rational. He presents a mathematical formalization of these ideas which circumscribes the set of rational "revision functions", while leaving open the possiblity of more detailed models making use of nonlogical factors. Gardenfors claims that the dynamics of such a belief system can serve as a theoretical basis for propositional logic, and have applications to problems about conditionals.
