Discussion of Michael Thielscher's "The Logic of Dynamic Systems" in IJCAI95

Khemdut Purang
University of Maryland

Abstract: In this paper, Thielscher presents a framework to represent and reason about dynamic systems. His theory handles concurrency, delayed effects and nondeterminism. A semantics for this theory is given. He also addresses the temporal projection problem, the planning problem and the postdiction problem within that framework. The paper is illustrated with variations on the Yale Shooting Problem.
