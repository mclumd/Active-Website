WEEKLY SEMINAR ON LOGIC AND AI

Tuesday, Oct. 24. 1995

TIME: 3:30 P.M. - 4:30 P.M.

PLACE: Room 3258 AVW Bldg. University of Maryland at College Park

SPEAKER: Dr. Richmond H. Thomason

University of Pittsburgh

TITLE: ``Intended Interpretation in Cooperative Discourse''

ABSTRACT:

The crucial problem in discourse is how speakers decide what to leave out of utterances, and how hearers decide what to put into the interpretation of utterances. I will argue that the most useful constraint on this process is that -- at least in relatively simple, task-related discourse -- what the speaker leaves out but means should equal what the hearer reconstructs. To see how this constraint can be satisfied, we need to simulate the whole discourse process: generation and interpretation. I will motivate this approach to discourse and describe what progress we (a group at SRI and The University of Pittsburgh) have achieved in pursuing it in a recent project.

Everyone is welcome to attend.
