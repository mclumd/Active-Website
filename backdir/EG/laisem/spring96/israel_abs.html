WEEKLY SEMINAR ON LOGIC AND AI

Tuesday, March 26, 1996

TIME: 3:30 P.M. - 4:30 P.M.

PLACE: Room 3258 AVW Bldg. University of Maryland at College Park

SPEAKER: David Israel

SRI International

TITLE: ``Theories and Logics of Action''

ABSTRACT:

There has been a recent rebirth of interest in `formal theories of action' in Artificial Intelligence (and in Philosophical Logic as well). Very little of the work in AI actually consists of theorizing about the nature of action. Most of it has been devoted to modeling certain central patterns of defeasible reasoning about actions; much of it within the Situation Calculus. The basic Situation Calculus does presuppose or present a model of actions as state transformers--in the basic case, functions from states to states. Slightly generalized, to relations between states, this is the almost unanimous view of action in Computer Science. (This view has played almost no role in Philosophy.) We present yet another theory of action, one which suggests a couple of different logics of action. In doing so, we waive aside all issues of defeasible reasoning. We mention one of these logics, and sketch the second, which is based on old ideas in Computer Science. We will also probably digress a lot on related theories and related logics.

Dr. Israel received the B.A. in Philosophy from Harvard College, and the Ph.D. in Philosophy from the University of California at Berkeley. Before joining SRI's Artificial Intelligence Center in 1984, Dr. Israel was a Senior Scientist at BBN Laboratories. Aside from his position as a Senior Computer Scientist in the AIC, he is a Consulting Professor in the Philosophy Department at Stanford University and Senior Research Associate at the Center for the Study of Language and Information at Stanford.
