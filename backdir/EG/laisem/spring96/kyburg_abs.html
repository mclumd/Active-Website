Tuesday, May 7, 1996

PLACE: Room 3258 AVW Bldg. University of Maryland at College Park

SPEAKER:

Alice Kyburg
University of Wisconsin -- Oshkosh

TITLE: ``Vague Utterances and Context Change''

ABSTRACT:

How can vague utterances be represented? And how do we update common knowledge when we interpret vague utterances? We will suggest a model of the context change required to accommodate vague utterances. It will account for the fact that the extensions of vague adjectives like "smart" and "fat" can be stretched to suit the purposes of the participants in a dialogue.

-----------------------------

Alice Kyburg is a visiting professor from the Philosophy Department of the University of Wisconsin -- Oshkosh. She is presenting some work done jointly with Professor Michael Morreau.

Everyone is welcome to attend.
