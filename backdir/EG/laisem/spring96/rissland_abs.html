WEEKLY SEMINAR ON LOGIC AND AI

Tuesday, March 12, 1996

TIME: 3:30 P.M. - 4:30 P.M.

PLACE: Room 3258 AVW Bldg. University of Maryland at College Park

SPEAKER:

Edwina Rissland
University of Massachusetts, Amherst

TITLE: ``Cases and Rules: A Natural Synergy''

ABSTRACT:

In this talk, I'll discuss the use of case-based reasoning (CBR) in concert with other reasoning paradigms, particularly, rule-based reasoning (RBR). I'll begin by examining the natural synergy between cases and rules, especially in situations where the rules and cases involve open-textured concepts, that is, concepts that cannot be universally defined by necessary and sufficient conditions. For such concepts, one typically resorts to cases. I'll then review the basic mechanisms of case-based reasoning, such as case indexing and selection. The main part of my talk will address CABARET, a hybrid CBR-RBR system built in my lab. CABARET combines RBR and CBR in a dynamic way using an agenda-based control architecture. The heuristics used to drive the system embody a three-tiered approach of argument stances, moves, and primitives to the problem of argument in domains with rules and cases, such as statutory law.

Everyone is welcome to attend.
