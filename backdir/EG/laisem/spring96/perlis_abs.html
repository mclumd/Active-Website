WEEKLY SEMINAR ON LOGIC AND AI

Tuesday, June 25, 1996

TIME: 4:00 P.M.

PLACE: Room 3258 AVW Bldg. University of Maryland at College Park

SPEAKER:

Don Perlis
University of Maryland at College Park

TITLE: ``Conversational Adequacy''

ABSTRACT:

We argue that meta-dialog and meta-reasoning, far from being of only occasional use, are the very essence of conversation and communication between agents. We give four paradigm examples of massive use of meta-dialog where only limited base dialog may be present, and use these to bolster our claim of centrality for meta-dialog. We further illustrate this with related work in active logics. We argue moreover that there may be a core set of meta-dialog principles that is in some sense complete. If we are right, then implementing such a set would be of considerable interest. We give examples of existing computer programs that converse inadequately according to our guidelines.

Everyone is welcome to attend.
