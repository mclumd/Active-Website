Inheritance Comes of Age: Applying Nonmonotonic Techniques to Problems in Industry

Leora Morgenstern
IBM T.J. Watson Research Center

Nonmonotonic reasoning is virtually absent from industry and has been since its inception; the result is that the field is becoming marginalized within AI. I argue that this is because researchers in the area focus exclusively on commonsense problems which are irrelevant to industry and because few efficient algorithms and tools have been developed. A sensible strategy is thus to focus on industry problems and to develop solutions within tractable subtheories of nonmonotonic logic.

I examine one of the few examples of nonmonotonic reasoning in industry --- inheritance of business rules in the medical insurance domain --- and show how the paradigm of inheritance can be extended to a broader and more powerful kind of nonmonotonic reasoning. This is done by introducing *formula-augmented semantic networks* (FANs), semantic networks which attach well-formed formulae to nodes. I investigate the concept of inheriting wffs in such networks and give a method for computing the sets of wffs that apply to a given node. I explore the connections between FANs and other forms of nonmonotonic reasoning, and consider which forms are most suitable for the application at hand. Finally, I discuss the underlying lessons that can be generalized to other problems in the commercial world.
