The Nixon example

Description:

This is the well known Nixon example. Alma knows that Nixon is both a republican (who are not pacifists) and a quaker (who are). Alma cannot decide whether Nixon is pacifist or not.

View the history applet
Alma specification

Run:

  • Alma start off with information as to how to handle contradictions and that repubicans are not pacifists, quakers are and nixon is both.
  • At step 2, Alma derives both that nixon is pacifist and that he is not.
  • At step 3, Alma asserts that there is a contradiction and distrusts the contradictands.
  • At step 4, Alma applies the contradiction handling axioms to try to resolve the problem.
  • But that gets nowhere because we did not provide any preference information. So Alma believes neither that nixon is a pacifist nor that he is not a pacifist.

Note:

If we were to later add that we prefer that "quakers are pacifists" to "republicans are not pacifists", then Alma will conclude that nixon is a pacifist.
