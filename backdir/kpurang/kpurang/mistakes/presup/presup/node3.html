Updating the context

There are three possibilities when we have a new utterance: (1) the context entails the presuppositions of the utterance; (2) the context entails the negation of the presuppositions; (3) the context entails neiter the presupposition nor its negation. Cases (1) and (3) seem relatively unproblematic. In (1), there is no special problem relating to presuppositions. In (3), we can accomodate the utterance by adding the presupposition to the context (assuming we know that the context does not entail the negation of the presupposition).
