Introduction

As a conversation proceeds, the new sentences of the participants is interpreted in the context of what has been mentioned earlier. The discourse context represents , for a participant in a dialog, what is being conveyed in the dialog. We expect the context to grow as the conversation proceeds and more information is available. The problem we consider here is a problematic aspect of the relationship between the discourse context and the presuppositions of a new utterance.
