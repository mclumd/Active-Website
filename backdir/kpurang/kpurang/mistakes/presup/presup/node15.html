Why does it work and Heim's does not?

The reason our approach gives better results intuitively than Heim's approach is that we do more careful reasoning about the mistake that occurs. Once an inconsistency appears in Heim's approach, the whole segment of the dialog that is involved is discarded. In our case, then we notice the inconcistency, we note that as a mistake and we find the cause of hte mistake and resolve it which allows us to continue processing the rest of the utterance.

In Heim's approach, the algorithm does not ``realize'' that there is a mistake. There is no special datastructure or procedure that comes into play when the mistake occurs. This makes it difficult to have anything but the simplest response to mistakes.
