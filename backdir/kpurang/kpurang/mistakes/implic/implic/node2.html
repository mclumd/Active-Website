What is implicature

Implicatures, roughly, are propositions that are implied by an utterance without being entailed by that utterance. If someone says ``John is in the yard or at work.'' an implicature owuld be that the speaker does not know exactly where John is. This proposition is not entailed by what the speaker said, but seems to follow from it if we assume that the speaker is not being evasive.
