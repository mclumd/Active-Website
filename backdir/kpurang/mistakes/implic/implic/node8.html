Example 2

(A) Kathy: Are the roses fresh?
(B) Bill: They are in the fridge.
(D) Bill: But they are old.

As in the first example, (B) implicates that the roses are fresh. But then (D) seems to implicate that they are not fresh. Kathy may then decide to go with either of the implications or none of them. This seems to depend on Kathy's beleif about the power of fridges to keep roses fresh. If she has no opinion on that, then she will have no opinion onthe freshenss of the roses either after this dialog.
