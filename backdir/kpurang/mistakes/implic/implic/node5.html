Conversational implicature

Grice then says what a conversational implicature is:

A man who, by (in, when) saying (or making as if to say) that p has implicated that q, may be said to have conversationally inokicated that q provided that (1) he is presumed to be observing the conversational maxims, or at least the cooperation principle; (2) the supposition that he is aware that, or thinks that, q is required in order to make his saying or making as if to say p (or doing so in those terms) consistent with the presumption; (3) the speaker thinks (and would expect the hearer to think that the speaker thinks) that it is within the competence of the hearer to work out, or grasp intuitively, that the supposition mentioned in (2) is required.
