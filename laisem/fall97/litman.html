Evaluating Spoken Dialogue Agents

Diane J. Litman
AT&T Labs - Research
Florham Park, NJ 07932

Recent advances in dialogue modeling, speech recognition, and natural language processing have made it possible to build spoken dialogue agents for a wide variety of applications. Potential benefits of such agents include remote or hands-free access, ease of use, naturalness, and greater efficiency of interaction. However, a critical obstacle to progress in this area is the lack of a general framework for evaluating and comparing the performance of different dialogue agents.

This talk presents PARADISE (PARAdigm for DIalogue System Evaluation), a general framework for evaluating spoken dialogue agents that integrates and enhances previous work. PARADISE decouples task requirements from an agent's dialogue behaviors, specifies the relative contribution of various task and dialogue factors to performance, and makes it possible to compare agents performing different tasks by normalizing for task complexity. The application of PARADISE to an experimental spoken dialogue agent that accesses train schedule information from the web will be discussed.
