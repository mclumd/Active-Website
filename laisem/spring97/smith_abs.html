TITLE: ``An Integrated Architecture for Natural Language Dialog Processing: Theory and Results''

ABSTRACT:

Early research in natural language dialog processing focused on isolated studies of important subproblems. We present results from our work on developing a model of dialog processing that integrates the following behaviors.

* problem solving to achieve a target goal
* subdialog completion and movement
* user modeling to determine appropriate interactions
* context-dependent input interpretation
* varying levels of computer initiative

In addition to describing the underlying theory, we illustrate the practical applicability of the model via a video demonstration of the "Circuit Fix-It Shop," a dialog system constructed according to this theory. Finally, we will discuss an extension of the model that deals with one aspect of dialog miscommunication---that of word misrecognition---and show how context-based verification subdialogs can effectively address this problem.

(Collaborators on this research include Alan Biermann, Ruth Day, Dania Egedi, Robin Gambill, Steve Gordon, Richard Hipp, Robert Hoggard, Shannon Pollard, and Robert Rodman)
