Title: "Simulated Deixis in NLVR - (Natural Language and Virtual Reality System"

John Gurney
Army Research Laboratory


Abstract:

In this talk I will explain how the Natural Language and Virtual Reality system implements a simulation of natural language understanding involving certain types of deixis. Computational implementation of deictic theory is, of course, not straightforward. This means that semantic theories of deixis, e.g., based on work by Kripke, Kaplan and Dretske leave some things unexplained, such as How do people do it?, and How is it possible? Short of answering these questions we use the NLVR system as a processing (as opposed to formal) model - showing how something perhaps like deictic understanding might be done in this instance.

A user of NLVR navigates his point of view through a virtual landscape in real-time by talking:

Start heading for the Tiefort Mountains
Look to your right
Go twice this fast
How far is that guy? [pointing at an object]
The third utterance uses deixis to a property; the last uses deixis to a perceived object - all simulated.

I will show some video tape first. Then I will concentrate on some of the relevant representational and computational details of the system.
