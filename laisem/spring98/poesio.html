Title: "Towards an Axiomatization of Dialogue Acts"

Massimo Poesio
University of Edinburgh


Abstract: Conversations involve all sorts of verbal activities beyond those strictly related to the performance of the task at hand. Among other things, the participants in a conversation have to make sure they both understand what's going on, to manage turn taking, and to keep each other informed about their progress in achieving their task. The participants share information about the status of all of these processes; this suggests that the view of the conversational score they share is more complex than traditionally assumed in models that concentrate on the propositional content of assertions, in that it involves both semantic and pragmatic information. In this talk I will present the latest developments in ongoing work towards a formal model of the conversational score which attempts to bridge the gap between theories developed in formal semantics and theories developed in Artificial Intelligence, but which takes into account all of these varieties of shared information. After a brief introduction to some of the building blocks of our theory, I will show how we are currently using our model to give a partial specification of the effect of the dialogue acts included in the Discourse Resource Initiative (DRI) dialogue act coding scheme. (this is joint work with David Traum)
