Tuesday, December 10, 1996

SPEAKER:

Nicholas Asher
University of Texas at Austin

TITLE: ``Computational Commonsense Geometry''

ABSTRACT:

In this talk I will explore ways of integrating geometrical information (distance and orientation) with the commonsense topology developed in an IJCAI 95 paper of mine and Laure Vieu's. I will begin by presenting the case why commonsense geometry isn't just Euclidean geometry or some other extant mathematical system. Ordinary language conveys geometrical information without making appeal to the basics of mathematical systems like Euclidean geometry--e.g., dimensionless points, surfaces without depth, etc. The growing literature on the semantics of spatial expressions provides us with some evidence that commonsense geometry is different from Euclidean geometry.

My approach will build on the first order framework for mereotopology alluded to above. Geometrical information will be encoded within a modal extension. More specificially, I will exploit the framework of two dimensional temporal logic as developed by Venema and others within the spatial realm to capture orientation information and qualitative distance in an integrated and I hope intuitive way. I then add an operation of addition of lengths to capture the triangle inequality.

Everyone is welcome to attend.
